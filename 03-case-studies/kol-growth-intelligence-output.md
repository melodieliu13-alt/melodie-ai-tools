# Input → Output · KOL growth intelligence

This is a sanitized example of the decision format produced by the workflow. It shows the reasoning without publishing the private tweet archive, account-level notes or report.

## Input

**Research question:** Is AI pulling attention away from crypto, and should that change how a growth team positions a crypto product?

The pipeline retrieved the relevant June material from the local KOL archive. The report then separated source types, compared agreement across sources and marked claims that the available data could not prove.

## Output

| Layer | Finding |
|---|---|
| Signal | AI was gaining attention across a wider technology and finance audience, while crypto-native discussion remained active inside its own audience. |
| Rejected interpretation | “AI is siphoning capital from crypto.” Attention data alone could not establish capital movement or causality. |
| Revised judgment | The stronger explanation was **audience mismatch**, not a single zero-sum attention market. The two audiences responded to different problems and promises. |
| Growth action | Keep crypto-native acquisition focused on utility and market relevance. Test AI-led positioning separately with broader technology and finance audiences instead of forcing one message across both. |
| Confidence | Directional, not causal. Useful for message testing; insufficient for a budget reallocation without campaign data. |
| What would change the judgment | Cross-audience conversion data showing the same users consistently choosing AI products over crypto products. |

## What the tool did — and what I did

The tool preserved and retrieved the source material. AI helped cluster repeated themes and draft an initial synthesis.

I rejected the first causal framing, separated attention from capital flow, defined the audience split and decided what the evidence was strong enough to change. The final output is not “what the tweets said”; it is a bounded action and the condition that could prove it wrong.

## Privacy boundary

The underlying archive contains collected public posts and private working analysis, so it is intentionally excluded. No account handles, verbatim posts, employer-confidential information or private feedback are reproduced here.
